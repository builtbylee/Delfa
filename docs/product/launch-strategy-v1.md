# Delfa Launch Strategy V1

- Version: `0.1.0`
- Date: `2026-03-09`
- Status: `Recommended launch and growth strategy`

## 1. Goal

Grow Delfa without breaking match quality.

The launch strategy must support:

- dense local supply
- strong first-match quality
- trust on the hard side of the marketplace
- measurable activation and retention

## 2. Core Principles

1. `Launch hyper-locally`
   Start with one city, not a country.

2. `Solve the hard side first`
   In straight markets, women are typically the side that determines whether the market feels safe and worth staying in.

3. `Use a controlled founding cohort`
   Quality of the initial user base matters more than raw signups.

4. `Make the product itself marketable`
   Delfa should grow through insight, trust, and outcomes rather than gimmicks.

5. `Expand only after density is proven`
   A wider geography with weak local density will hurt the product.

## 3. Phase 0: Prelaunch

## 3.1 Waitlist wedge

Do not market Delfa as generic early access.

Use:

- a shortened Basics experience
- a private mirror-moment style insight
- waitlist capture after the insight

This gives:

- immediate single-player value
- a reason to share
- early segmentation by city, age band, and relationship intent

## 3.2 Content strategy

Lead with research-backed relationship content, not generic dating-app ads.

Good formats:

- short-form videos on what actually predicts lasting relationships
- profiler-question explainers
- posts on why current apps fail serious daters
- anonymized aggregate insights from the profiler once user volume exists

## 4. Phase 1: Founding Cohort

Recruit a controlled early cohort in one city.

Required qualities:

- relationship-minded
- in a compatible age band
- active and ready to date
- likely to respond and give feedback

Acquisition channels:

- founder network
- trusted referrals
- small ambassador cohort
- aligned communities

Aligned communities include:

- women’s professional networks
- relationship and mental-health content audiences
- book clubs and intentional social groups
- podcast communities in dating and relationships

## 5. Phase 2: City Launch

Launch in one city only.

City criteria:

- dense population of relationship-minded users
- realistic local date viability
- strong founder reach or partnership reach
- cultural fit for a premium intentional dating product

Within the city, Delfa should still prioritize local density over city-wide spread.

## 6. Product-Led Growth Mechanics

The product should create organic distribution through:

- mirror-moment style insights worth talking about
- distinctive profiler questions
- match explanations that feel smarter than swipe apps
- eventual success stories with genuine substance

Referral design should not be a generic discount loop.

Better structure:

- invite a friend who is also serious about dating
- both get a useful compatibility or relationship-style insight

## 7. Activation Metric

Delfa must identify the one early behavior that predicts retention.

Candidate activation sequence:

1. completes Basics
2. receives first high-quality match
3. completes round 1 guided interaction
4. reaches mutual ready-to-meet or meaningful continued conversation

Instrumentation should be built from day one so Delfa can discover which exact step best predicts retention.

## 8. Expansion Rules

Do not expand to a second city until the first one is clearly working.

Required signals:

- first matches arrive within an acceptable quality-protected window
- organic signups are increasing
- the hard side of the marketplace is staying active
- first-match conversation rates are strong
- ready-to-meet and date-conversion rates are healthy
- trust and safety load is manageable

## 9. What To Avoid

- national launch too early
- broad paid acquisition before local density works
- weak onboarding cohorts that produce junk inventory
- forcing growth through events when the core product is meant to be in-app
- optimizing on downloads instead of activation and outcome metrics

## 10. Summary

Recommended launch shape:

- one city
- one clear age band
- one relationship-minded user wedge
- controlled founding cohort
- waitlist led by profiler insight
- product-led sharing
- expand only after the first city proves density and outcomes
